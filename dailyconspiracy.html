JFK Assassination Controversy


This week we will be delving into one of the most popular events that draw numerous conspiracies, the assassination of President John F. Kennedy on November 22nd, 1963. Now the general belief is that he was assasinated while riding in a convertable through a parade in Dallas. Records say the deed was done by Lee Harvey Oswald, who fired at Kennedy from a window in a nearby building. The main suspicion from claims that the three shots fired at Kennedy came from different directions. The main claim was from a police officer claiming that there were shots fired from a nearby, now famous, "grassy knoll."\ These contrasting firing directions suggest there might be multiple shooters. It is also well known that Lee Harvey Oswald never testified in court due to him being killed by Jack Ruby the next day. It is officialy documented that Jack Ruby and Lee Harvey Oswald did know of eachother, and it was very possible that Jack Ruby had mafia ties being the owner of a shady club,as well as the Kennedy administration having focused on ending organized crime. Which would make sense that Ruby wouldkill Oswald to keep him from ratting him out. But is that worth the life sentence Ruby got?
